    Reagents

    Phosphoric Acid (104%)

    1. crystalline ortho-phosphoric acid (99%, ρ = 1.87, Sigma-Aldrich 1005650500) in water bath
    2. close valve, connect acid flask (round, otherwise implosion) to the glass manifolds
    3. ‘U’ & spiral water traps (LN2 in blue dewars) & 1 contamination (e.g., oil from vacuum pump) trap (LN2 in black bucket)
    4. open valve (vacuum)
    5. stirring (200 RPM) & boiling water bath (250 °C) for 6 h ⟹ 104%, ρ = 1.93
      • use a safety screen
      • replenish water & LN2 every 30 mins
    6. finish
      1. stop heating, lower water bath, cool off
      2. wipe off water around the connection, release vacuum, disconnect
      3. next day: disconnect and dry glass condensate traps

    References: Burman 2005

    CO2 ref gas

    Set up

    1. fill dewar on pump trap with LN2
    2. put carbonate and stirrer bar in reaction vessel
    3. top up acid reservoir with orthophosphoric acid (104%)
    4. fill dewars on ‘U’ & spiral water traps with ethanol (−80 °C)
    5. heat water bath to 90 °C (to avoid temperature fractionation of oxygen)
    6. connect ref gas cylinder to line and pump
    7. pump whole line down to 1e-3 mbar

    Manufacture

    1. close ref gas valve
    2. close valve to pump, check for leaks
    3. fill dewar on ‘U’ CO2 trap with LN2
    4. slowly drop acid and turn on stirrer
    5. vac gauge will rise the fall as produced CO2 is frozen down
    6. steadily add more acid
    7. reaction takes 45 mins to 1 hour
    8. close off valve to reaction vessel (open vessel vent)
    9. vacuum should drop back to 1e-3 (ideally!)

    10. pump over frozen CO2 to remove any uncondensables

    11. isolate line from pump and send CO2 back over the water traps

    12. put LN2 dewar on ‘carrot’ CO2 trap
    13. remove LN2 from ‘U’ CO2 trap, CO2 will refreeze on ‘carrot’ CO2 trap
    14. vacuum should drop back to 1e-3 (ideally!)

    15. pump over frozen CO2 to remove any uncondensables

    16. isolate line from pump and send CO2 back over the water traps

    17. put LN2 dewar on ‘U’ CO2 trap
    18. remove LN2 from ‘carrot’ CO2 trap, CO2 will refreeze on ‘U’ CO2 trap
    19. vacuum should drop back to 1e-3
    20. pump over frozen CO2 to remove any uncondensables
    21. isolate water traps form frozen CO2 trap
    22. open ref gas cylinder valve
    23. isolate line from vac pump
    24. fill ref gas dewar with LN2
    25. remove LN2 from ‘U’ CO2 trap
    26. freeze ref gas into cylinder
    27. vacuum should drop back to 1e-3
    28. pump over frozen CO2 to remove any uncondensables
    29. close ref gas cylinder, remove from line and allow to warm up.

    Notes

    expansion volume and second carrot trap is for ‘emergency’. It is a pre-evacuated 5 L volume that CO2 can be allowed to expand into in case of any problems.

    100 g CaCO3 = 22.4 L CO2 gas @ room temp (1 atm)

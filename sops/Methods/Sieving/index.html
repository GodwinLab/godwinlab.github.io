    Sieving

    Sieving Expedition 397 U1385 samples. Work in stratigraphic sequence.

    1. Disaggregation
      1. Take samples from a big plastic bag and sort them into cardboard boxes
      2. Take out samples according to labels prepared by Simon, put other samples into 2 mid-size plastic bags and then put back into the original big plastic bag
      3. Label (A, plastic, small) wide neck 250 ml conical flask
        • Put Label (A) on white electrical tape, and then put white electrical tape onto flask, so that Label (A, together with electrical tape) can be easily detached later
      4. Place flask onto the scale, tare
      5. Break samples into >3 parts and put into flask, weight of bulk sample
        • Keep a small piece of spare sample in the original plastic bag and seal with a heat sealer
      6. Add ~100 ml de-ionised (DI) water (almost, but do not fully submerge sample)
      7. Put on orange rubber bung
        • Simon & Jeannie: marked with a blue dot
        • DO NOT push down
      8. Put onto orbital shaker set at close to 200 rotations
        1. After 10 minutes, check subsamples are moving (detached from base) in beaker. If a lump is “stuck”, usually in the middle, try to move it to the side. Adjust speed as needed — different samples have different lowest working speeds.
        2. Leave for <4–5 hours. Shorter period is preferable, as long as samples reach a soupy slurry state. Higher rotations or longer period may crush or damage tests, esp. when there are big pyrites.
    2. Sieve
      1. Label (B, paper, big) plastic resealable bag
      2. write weight of resealable bag onto itself
        • Put a tape roll as a holder onto the balance
      3. Open resealable bag and put into 1000 mL (reusable) beaker
      4. Move Label (A) on flask to sieve
      5. Put 150 µm mesh ⌀100 mm stainless steel sieve on top of beaker
      6. Washout soupy slurry in flask with DI water, use higher flow rate (loosen spray nozzle)
      7. Sieve with DI water, use lower flow rate, i.e., fine aerosol (tighten spray nozzle)
        • may need one additional 1000 ml CLEAN beaker for fine fraction when there are big lumps
      8. Put flasks onto trolley for later wash by glassware washer
    3. Coarse fraction (foraminifera) on sieve:
      1. Put into oven at 40 °C, >1 hour until completely dry
      2. Take off Label (A) on sieve, trim it with a scissor, and then put it onto glass vial as a backup
      3. Label (C, plastic, small) glass vial
      4. Clean up triangle cardboard holder & metal funnel with brush
      5. Put triangle cardboard holder on to the scale
      6. Transfer coarse fraction from sieve to cardboard holder with two hard brushes (one made from tooth brush, mainly brush underneath the sieve; another No. 10 flat brush, brush inside the sieve and for transfer). dry weight (KERN PCB350-3)
      7. Transfer into glass vial with a metal funnel and a soft round No.4 brush
      8. Write sample info on the cap of glass vial
    4. Fine fraction (coccoliths, nannofossils, etc.) in resealable bag (in beaker):
      1. Settle, 3 or 4 days until liquid is clear
      2. Drain by pump as much as possible, without disturbing fine particles
      3. Optional: transfer the portion from the additional beaker to the resealable bag (rinse and transfer), settle–drain
      4. Dry in 50 °C oven
      5. Sort on a desk
      6. dry weight and weight of resealable bag
        • Put a tape roll as a holder onto the balance
      7. Put into a big bag
        • Gather samples from the same section into one big plastic resealable bag
      8. Google spreadsheet: SedLab Copy of U1385 CSF-A and CCSF depths for BCR samples

    Notes

    • Samples are prepared in order, starting from the top of a core and working downwards. This minimises any errors with labelling through out the whole process.
    • In some cases sieve may need to be sonicated a few seconds to remove the coarse fraction trapped in the mesh.
    • Contents of water and coarse fraction can be calculated from recorded weights.

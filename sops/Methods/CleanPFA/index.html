    Clean PFA or FEP wares

    1. Initial cleaning:
      • Put on clean nitrile gloves.
      • Use ethanol1 (Analytical Reagent, E/0650DF/XX) on a tissue to wipe off the marks.
      • Empty contents and rinse both the inside and outside of the beaker under a slow flow of tap water.
    2. Detergent cleaning:
      • Pour Decon 90 detergent into one beaker, use Kimwipe to gently wipe (DO NOT scratch the smooth surface) the inside of beakers.
      • The Kimwipe soaked with Decon 90 can be used to wipe several beakers until it becomes too dry for effective cleaning.
      • Use Kimwipe soaked with Decon 90 to gently wipe the inside of caps.
      • Rinse with tap water one by one, and place into 1 L PFA jar(s). Separate bottles and caps into different jars.
    3. Rinse with Milli-Q water (MQ):
      • Fill 1 L PFA jar with MQ.
      • Close jar, shake it.
      • Open jar, pour out water. Use the cap to prevent the PFA wares from spilling.
      • Repeat shaking and pouring 2–3 times to thoroughly empty the water.
      • Repeat rinsing 3–4 times.
    4. Acetone cleaning:
      • Add ~200 mL acetone (Analytical Reagent, A/0600/XX) to a 1 L PFA jar.
      • Close jar, shake it.
      • Open jar (in a fume hood), pour acetone into the next jar with PFA wares to be cleaned.
      • Rinse with MQ (Step 3)
    5. HCl cleaning:
      • Add 50% (v/v, ≈6 M) HCl (Analytical Reagent, 20252.420) to 1 L PFA jar.
      • Close jar, shake it.
      • Open jar (in a fume hood), ensure all PFA wares are immersed.
      • Put on a hotplate2 at 140 °C for >1 h & <overnight.
      • Rinsing with MQ (Step 3)
    6. HNO3 cleaning:
      • Repeat Step 5, but use 50% (v/v, ≈8 M) HNO3 (Analytical Reagent, 20422.322) for >48 h & <72 h.
    7. Dilute HNO3 cleaning:
      • Repeat step 5, but use 1% 1Δ3 HNO3 for ~24h.
    8. Air dry:
      • Lay some big Kimwipes (Kimberly-Clark™ 7551) in a laminar flow hood4.
      • Place PFA wares on Kimwipes, and cover them with additional Kimwipes. You can use clean closed beakers to secure the covering Kimwipes in place.
      • Wait until dry. Put closed beakers into green PP trays and then stack them in specific plastic draws5.
    9. Pre-use check and cleaning:
      • Check the inner surface of beaker, if there are scratches, do not use it, put into the PFA ware “graveyard” box.
      • Fill ~⅓ of the capacity with 50% (v/v) AR HCl.
      • Put on a hotplate at 140 °C for >1h.
      • Rinse the inside of both beakers and caps with MQ 3 times before use.

    1. For stubborn marks, acetone can be used. ↩

    2. DO NOT drag things on a Teflon™-coated hotplate. ↩

    3. 1Δ means distilled once. ↩

    4. Use the specific hood for drying, otherwise set “EXHAUST” to 0, “CLEAN AIR” to 1. ↩

    5. There are “normal”, “low blank”, “high blank” draws, and personal/project draws. Discuss with PI and/or lab manager before you use PFA beakers. ↩

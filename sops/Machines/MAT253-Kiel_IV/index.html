    Procedures

    1. Monday: reboot computer

      • icon on the taskbar: Shutdown (may be grey)
      • reboot computer
      • Kiel IV Carbonate: Turn on “Oven control” & check JUMO temperature controller light on the Front Panel

    2. Acid drop test

    3. LN2

      1. Take off blue LN2 bucket

      2. Check LN2 cylinder

        • 15 samples & 8 standards: consumes ~15%

      3. Moving

        • pressure inside vessel < 50% of the relief valve level
        • relief valve orientated away from face

      4. Change LN2 cylinder

        1. disconnect electric cable of the cryogenic solenoid valve (ASCO SCE263-205LT)
        2. disconnect Teflon hose (small nut)
          • !! hold the neighbouring male thread stud with a spanner to prevent from loosening or over-tightening
        3. disconnect cryogenic solenoid valve (big nut)
        4. connect: seal big nut with PTFE film, 3 times
        5. fully open outlet valve on LN2 cylinder, and then turn it back one full rotation

    4. Grease Kiel’s vials, insert disposable vials into Kiel’s vials

    5. Clear acid pump

      1. Acquisition – Kiel IV Carbonate: right Take magazine, until Ready...
      2. wipe off acid
      3. stick paper into the circular space around the needles to clean
      4. Monday: take off O-ring, clean, check, (replace), grease
      5. check alignment of needle and sensor
      6. load empties/standards/samples
        • pos 1 for line 1, 2: empty vial
        • pos 2 for line 2: empty vial when using line 1 only sequence
      7. Kiel IV Carbonate: right Load magazine

    6. Check JUMO temperature controller

    7. Edit sequence in Acquisition (CO2_KielIV_Godwin_*.seq)

      1. input Identifier 1 for samples, Weight for standards
      2. Options: update date in the Folder Name and Export File Name
      3. Save
      4. Put on a dry blue LN2 bucket
      5. Start
